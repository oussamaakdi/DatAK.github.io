Building a multiclass classifier using CNNs for image classification

Description

In this project, we've constructed a multiclass classifier using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for image classification. CNNs are a type of deep learning model widely employed in computer vision tasks due to their remarkable ability to automatically extract features from images. These features, learned through convolutional layers, enable CNNs to classify images accurately.

Understanding CNNs and Kernels

CNNs are inspired by the human visual system, with layers of neurons that progressively analyze an image. Convolutional layers employ filters, also known as kernels, to scan the image. These kernels detect features like edges, shapes, and textures, gradually forming a rich understanding of the image content. In our project, we utilize the power of kernels to recognize and classify fruits.

Creating and Cleaning the Dataset

To train our model, we first needed a dataset. In the Useful Materials section , we've provided guidance on obtaining an image dataset. Our dataset consists of three classes: "banana," "strawberry," and "apple." The process involved cleaning the dataset by removing incompatible images and ensuring data consistency.

Data Preprocessing

Data preprocessing is a crucial step. We divided the dataset into manageable batches, then further partitioned it into training, validation, and test datasets. This division ensures that our model learns effectively and can generalize to unseen data.

Model Architecture

Our model is a sequential neural network with multiple layers. It includes a series of consecutive convolutional layers and pooling layers. These layers work together to automatically learn essential features from the images, enabling accurate classification.

Evaluation and Performance

After training the model, we evaluated its performance using the cross-entropy metric. The evaluation allowed us to understand how well the model classifies the fruit images and provided insights into its accuracy and generalization capabilities.
